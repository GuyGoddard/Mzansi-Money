Business Registration Guide

Register your business with CIPC, access funding, and comply with tax — all in one guide.

4 Simple Steps to Register

1
Reserve Your Name

Think of 4 name options. Go to bizportal.gov.za and search if your name is available. Reserve it for R50. Name valid for 6 months.

2
Register with CIPC

Complete registration at cipc.co.za or bizportal.gov.za. Cost: R125 (Pty Ltd). You need SA ID, proof of address, and your directors' details.

Open CIPC Checklist chevron_right
3
Tax Registration (SARS)

Your company is automatically registered for tax when CIPC registration is approved. SARS will send you a tax certificate. Register for VAT if you exceed R1 million turnover/year.

4
Open a Business Account

Keep personal and business money separate. FNB Business Zero, Capitec Business, Nedbank Business, or Standard Bank all offer entry-level accounts.

✓ Visit your nearest SEDFA office for free business support

Tax & Compliance for Businesses

PAYE — Pay As You Earn

If you pay employees, you must register for PAYE and deduct income tax from salaries. Submit EMP201 monthly to SARS.

VAT — Value Added Tax

Register for VAT when your turnover exceeds R1 million/year. You can voluntarily register from R50,000/year. VAT rate is 15%.

UIF & SDL Contributions

Register all employees for UIF (1% employee + 1% employer). If payroll exceeds R500,000/year, pay 1% Skills Development Levy (SDL).

Provisional Tax

If you expect taxable income over R30,000 beyond what your employer withholds, you are a provisional taxpayer. Pay twice yearly: Aug 31 and Feb 28.

Annual Returns to CIPC

File annual returns with CIPC within 30 business days of your anniversary date. Costs R100–R3,000 depending on turnover. Non-compliance leads to deregistration.
